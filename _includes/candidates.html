Michelle Esposito for Vice Chair

Program Manager 2, Platforms Pillar

I became a shop steward in April 2022 and a "steward little" since early 2021. I now belong to several volunteer committees including the Member Data, Union Onboarding, and Stewards Network Overhaul working groups.

My project management skills; the organizing of people and teams toward a common goal, are basically directly transferable to union organizing. This is why I enjoy this work so much - it is essentially my career and life's work.

My crowning organizing achievement to date was coordinating with stewards across our unit to empower members and collect a supermajority of signatures on our Strike Authorization Vote Pledge. This enabled us to schedule a vote with an confidently strong outcome. I knew a strong vote in favor to strike (95%) would finally get us a compensation counter from management and it did.

I've also led several successful actions, notably organizing team leads of Elections Readiness critical teams to use union-supportive backgrounds for the August Stress Test.

My priorities include:

  • Mobilizing and keeping the stewards' network accountable to our members and strong in our support of members
  • Organizing and processing member data to keep an accurate pulse on the strength of our unit
  • Onboarding and welcoming new members, empowering them to participate in our union!

In addition to organizing for our unit I also volunteer on my building's co-op board and I mentor a young student in college. My other hobbies and personal interests include playing cards with friends, pub trivia, karaoke, horror movies, dancing, fishing, scuba diving, and skiing. I also enjoy traveling and have aimed to visit one new country per year. This year was Japan, next year I don't know yet. Open to suggestions!

Vicki Crosson for Secretary

Sr Engineer in DevP - Remote from Boston

I'm the current secretary of Times Tech, and I'm running for another term. My lived experience as an autistic/ADHD wheelchair user shapes my belief that solidarity means protecting everyone.

My union involvement started with our underground organizing committee, and I've been deeply engaged ever since. I served on the bargaining committee, where I stood across the table from management at its most combative — I'm ready to do it again.

One of the most powerful actions I've been part of was the RTO pledge. When the company threatened illegal discipline during status quo, we pledged that if one person was punished, we'd all walk out. We were ready, and management backed down. That's where I see our solidarity shine: any of us can become “the little guy” in an instant, so we have to stand strong together for everyone, every time.

As secretary, I've built and maintained the systems that keep our large unit running, managed the strike fund, ensured rallies and pickets are accessible as possible, onboarded new members, and served on the Grievance Committee fighting for members' rights. Beyond our unit, I've pushed the NYGuild executive committee to take stronger stances as NYGuild secretary, built solidarity by exchanging proposals with other tech unions, spoken on panels about disability justice, supported new organizing drives (including Wirecutter/TA tech), and helped establish a Local emergency fund.

My priorities for the future:

  • Protecting members from retaliation and illegal discipline
  • Preserving institutional memory and ensuring organizational continuity
  • Expanding accessibility and centering disability justice
  • Elevating minority voices and making sure decisions are led by impacted members — “nothing about us without us”
  • Building solidarity across units and industries so our collective power grows stronger

I'm not here to push my own agenda — I'm here to make sure we protect each other, stay organized, and build the power we need to win.

I enjoy quilting, language learning (Russian, Japanese, Spanish & Quechua so far), and serving on my town's disability commission. I was a street medic, and still maintain my EMT license. I earned a Library & Information Science MS (while bargaining!), and I love scary books, women's hockey, musicals, and trying to keep our two cats — Maz (pictured) and Roger Roger — busy. Fun fact: I had a Star Wars wedding, and I sewed my own dress!
