Timothy Kurtz - A Unit Chair for All

Our union is at a crossroads. We've built strength, won important protections in our first contract, and pushed back when management tried to divide us. But we need to grow stronger and that means changing how we operate, how we organize, and how we lead.

I'm running for Unit Chair of the Times Tech Guild to transform the status quo. I believe our union should be a place where all members, even those who are not organizers, feel informed, included and empowered.

Who Am I?

I've been a shop steward since September 2022, just three months after joining The Times, which meant I was learning my job and how to organize at the same time (I'm a notorious multitasker, lol). I started by organizing my own team, then grew into chief steward for Digital Ads as well as Engineering in Growth and in Data. If you're in any of those three missions, I can guarantee I've checked in on you, either personally or through your steward, more than once over the last three years.

I've served on several article committees, always making sure my members' interests were reflected in our proposals. I co-lead our Contract Action Team, planned our first real walkout on Halloween 2023, and during our strike a year later mobilized 100% Digital Ads withheld their labor, with many leading on both the digital or physical picket lines. My members show up and fight alongside me because they know I show up and fight for them, and I've taken that fight straight to our CTO, Jason, and our CEO, Meredith, to stop members from being fired or forced out. Currently, I sit on our remote work committee assisting members with applications and appeals.

Outside of organizing, I'm a devoted fan of Paradox Interactive, board games, Magic: The Gathering (ask me about my Lightning Bolts) and, most importantly, my wife Maria.

Here's what I'll bring to our leadership

Transparency and Communication

As the lead steward in Digital Ads, Growth and Data Platforms during our contract campaign, I worked my hardest to represent my members in article committees, collective actions, and in planning our strike. But despite this, there were still many gaps in communication at every level, both between our BC and our stewards as well as between our stewards and our rank and file members. As Unit Chair I will make it a top priority to communicate clearly and regularly with stewards and members about our decisions, our goals, and our strategy. Rank and file members shouldn't have to know someone on the inside in order to get help or start participating, and organizers shouldn't have to guess about overall strategy. Misalignment and confusion weakens our actions and costs us wins.

A Steward Network to Empower Members

Stewards shouldn't feel like another boss, they should be your advocates, educators, and organizers. Every rank and file member deserves to have such a steward who they trust to both defend their jobs and represent their interests, not just those fortunate enough to be in particular missions or functions that happen to have more organizers with time or capacity for volunteering. Since winning our contract, I have already begun working to revitalize our steward network, and rebuild it to be consistent, responsive, and accountable. As Unit Chair, I intend to work hard to train, support, and develop new stewards across our unit in all areas of the company: bringing in new leaders and giving them real ownership over organizing.

I remember what it felt like to be a newly engaged member, eager to help but having to figure out for myself how best to meaningfully contribute. I remember the first time as a steward where a concern of mine was pushed aside by those with louder voices. In my time as a Steward, I have put great effort into making sure any member feels welcome and taken seriously in any organizing space whenever they decide to pitch in. As Unit Chair, we will organize not just with an open door, but with an outstretched hand creating real space for new people to step in, lead, and grow.

Member-Led Bargaining

We can't fight for what our members truly need if we don't ask them and listen. Our future contract campaigns must be built from the ground up, rooted in what members actually want: better compensation, stronger job protections, more time back, and fairer benefits. Stewards being surprised by member priorities during a contract campaign costs us wins and weakens our credibility bargaining on behalf of our unit. Further, we need leadership that rank and file members trust to truly represent their interests. That means a process that engages all members of our unit, not just in selecting our bargaining committee's priorities, but also the committee itself.

Here's what I've already done and what I'll keep building on:

Inspired members who had never been involved before to take on organizing projects, join campaigns, and advocate for themselves and their coworkers

Helped recruit, train, and support aspiring stewards and new organizers

Pushed for greater accountability and structure in steward work and built the team-based stewardship model we need to mobilize members and win

Here's where we're going next, together:

  • A stronger steward network that ensures no unit member is left behind, no matter where they work or what their issue is
  • A contract enforcement strategy that protects every member's rights and prevents management from exploiting gaps in our coverage
  • Coalition-building across our union and beyond, we need to win the fight to include our members in The Athletic, Wirecutter, the Design function, and elsewhere in the company, to prevent management from chipping away our power. We must fight our work being displaced and members laid off, and reject management's attempts to silo us.
  • A member-driven process for choosing our leadership, so that our leadership reflects the full strength and diversity of our unit and is accountable to the people we serve
