Timothy Kurtz - A Unit Chair for All

Our union is at a crossroads. We've built strength, won important protections in our first contract, and pushed back when management tried to divide us. But we need to grow stronger to enforce our contract and that means changing how we operate, how we organize, and how we lead.

I'm running for Unit Chair of the Times Tech Guild to transform our union into one that truly represents and defends our rank and file. I believe our union should be a place where all members, even those who are not organizers, feel included, empowered, and protected.

Who Am I?

I became a steward in September 2022, only three months after joining The Times. Back then, I was learning my job and how to organize at the same time (I'm a notorious multitasker, lol). I started organizing my own team and later became the lead steward for Digital Ads and Engineering in Data Platforms and Growth. If you’re in those missions, I can guarantee I've checked in on you, either personally or through your steward.

When bargaining started for our first contract, I made the decision to join several article committees to ensure my members’ priorities were represented. When management unlawfully threatened to enforce discipline over RTO during our contract campaign, I worked to plan our first ULP strike on Halloween 2023. Soon after, I became a co-lead of our Contract Action Team, organizing collective actions to move us to a contract. I quickly put that experience to work in my steward responsibilities, organizing smaller team-based actions to protect members. During our strike, I mobilized 100% of Digital Ads to withhold their labor, with many taking leadership positions on the picket lines (both digital and in person). Members fight alongside me because they know I will always fight for them.

Since our strike, I've continued fighting for members, even escalating certain cases directly to our CTO and CEO when members were being forced out. Currently, I serve on our Remote Work Contract Committee assisting members applying for remote work, and advocating for them in appeals and arbitration.

Outside of organizing, I'm a devoted fan of Paradox Interactive, board games, Magic: The Gathering (ask me about my Lightning Bolts) and, most importantly, my wife Maria.

Here's what I'll bring to our leadership

Stronger Defense for Members and Our Contract

My priority has always been defending members when management mistreats them. Even before we had a contract, I would organize teams to protect coworkers from management’s worst decisions, but without guaranteed job protections, the strength of those strategies were limited. Now with the Just Cause protections, contract committees, and grievance provisions provided by our contract, we have powerful new tools to challenge management when they mistreat members. As Unit Chair, I plan to use my experience enforcing our contract and organizing teams to build a union capable of resisting management on multiple fronts. You’ll never doubt whether your union will have your back.

We also need a leader who will block management’s attempts to weaken our contract. In just our first year under contract, we’ve seen their attempts to clawback benefits and erode our rights by reinterpreting layoff protections or circumventing our contract committee processes. As Unit Chair, I will be relentless when pursuing grievances on your behalf, and even in settlements, will make things as expensive as possible for management. So they think twice before violating our contract again.

A Steward Network to Empower Members

Your Steward shouldn't feel like a second boss, they should be your advocates, educators, and organizers. Every member deserves that support, not just those fortunate enough to be in particular missions or functions where there happen to be more volunteers. Since we won our contract, I have already begun working to revitalize and rebuild our steward network to be consistent, responsive, and accountable. This is how we will build community and enforce our contract everywhere across our unit.

I remember what it felt like to be a newly engaged member, eager to help but having to figure out for myself how best to meaningfully contribute. I even remember the first time as a steward where concerns I had for members of my team were pushed aside by those with louder voices who viewed the issue as "too small". As Unit Chair, I intend to put great effort into ensuring that any member, no matter how new, feels welcome in our organizing spaces, and that stewards are always supported in advocating for the concerns of teams and individual members.

Open Communication

As a former lead steward, I saw how gaps in communication weakened us. Decisions didn’t always flow clearly between our bargaining committee, stewards, and members. As your Unit Chair, I will make it a top priority to communicate clearly and regularly with stewards and members about our decisions, our goals, and our strategy. You shouldn’t have to know someone on the inside to get help or start participating, and organizers shouldn’t be left guessing at our plans. Opaque decisions and poor communication erodes trust and creates misalignment, which weakens participation and makes it harder to push back against management.

Communication must also be two-way. We win only when we fight for what members care about and our future actions and contract campaigns must be rooted in members real priorities: better compensation, stronger job protections, more time back, and fairer benefits. Stewards being surprised by member priorities during a contract campaign costs us wins and weakens our credibility, as does choosing a bargaining committee without the input of our rank and file. As unit chair, your priorities will be mine as well.

Here's what I've already done, and what I'll keep building on:

Protected members from discipline, and helped members win substantial settlements from management both with and without a contract

Recruited, trained, and supported aspiring stewards and new organizers

Pushed for greater accountability and structure in steward work and built the team-based stewardship model we need to mobilize members and win

Mobilized entire teams and missions to take action to defend members and win our contract

Here's where we're going next, together:

  • A contract enforcement strategy that protects every member and prevents management from exploiting gaps in our coverage, or interpreting away members’ rights
  • A stronger steward network that ensures no unit member is left behind, no matter where they work or what their issue is
  • Transparent communication, and member led priorities, so that we can stay moving forward together toward goals that members actually need
  • Coalition-building across our union and beyond to win uncontested inclusion of our members in Design, The Athletic, Wirecutter, and elsewhere in the company. We will reject management’s attempts to silo us and cut off the avenues they use to displace our work: deceptive job titles, shuffing departments, or contracting out of our unit.
